About

Magdeline Tshepiso Rebatenne started playing table tennis back in 2000 at Letlole Mosielele school in Thamaga when she was doing form 1. Her first introduction to table tennis was through her English teacher, and that is when her love for sport started. In the same year she was selected to be part of the table tennis school team and she managed to reach the nationals and she was the only player who qualified for that stage.


Her love for table tennis continued as she played for senior school team though she did not win any trophies during the 3 years she was playing for senior category but she did not give up on the sport. After she finishes her studies at senior secondary school she had to join outside clubs to keep fit, and from 2005 she did not have game time until she resumed playing table tennis again in 2008 when she was playing her first Open table tennis tournament in Orapa. She later went to university where she was selected to play for Botho University table tennis team, where she won her first table tennis medal, and she was awarded the best graduating sports person in 2012 when she was graduating.

Club Career

Tshepiso in action, bottom left corner

Magdline's lack of game time at and fitness level was worrisome as most of the local table tennis clubs did not want to sign her as their player. She later met Molepolole originated players and decided to form their own club, even though the idea was good but the club formation did not materialize. She started her regular training at the University of Botswana where she met players of high caliber that helped her improve her skills.


During her training at the University of Botswana, Magdeline finally found a mentor who helped her with training plans. She started participating in open tournaments locally and Botswana Defence Force (BDF) signed her as their female table tennis player. She played for Botswana Defence Force for 4 years. During her stay at BDF she rose to fame and reached her performance peak and she started challenging for championship in the ladies category on every tournament locally and in the Southern Africa himisphere. After 4 years she joined BDF rivals Nhabe table tennis club in 2015 where she is still playing at to date.


International Career

See Also: BTAA national team selection

Tshepiso was part of the Botswana table tennis national team that qualified in the World Team Table Tennis Championships that were held in Tokyo, Japan in 2014. She made history by being part of the team that competed in the WTTC since it was the first time Botswana took part in the competion.

In 2014 Tshepiso Rebatenne was part of the team that qualified for the Commonwealth Games held in Glasgow, Scotland in 2014, before Commonwealth Games Tshepiso and her team recieved blessings by meeting and having lunch with Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom in July 2014 as well as a launch meeting by the former president of the Republic Of Botswana, Lieutenant General Seretse Khama Ian Khama.

The year 2014 was full of events for the Botswana star, she won a bronze medal with the Botswana table tennis national team at the Zone VI Regional Table Tennis Championships in Zambia, Lusaka in 2014.
